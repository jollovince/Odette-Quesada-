ABOUT

Odette Quesada is a legendary Filipina singer-songwriter whose music has defined the sound of love and romance in Original Pilipino Music (OPM). From the very beginning of her career in the 1980s, she established herself as a prolific songwriter with an exceptional gift for weaving heartfelt lyrics into timeless melodies.

Odette Quesada first gained national attention when she won second prize in the 1982 Metro Manila Popular Music Festival (Metropop) amateur division for her song “Give Me a Chance”, performed by Ric Segreto. In 1983, she followed this with a monumental achievement—clinching the Grand Prize in the professional category at the 6th Metropop for “Till I Met You”, sung by Kuh Ledesma.

The 1980s became a golden era for the music of Odette Quesada, as her songs were brought to life by some of the country’s biggest and brightest stars. The late Ric Segreto turned “Don’t Know What to Do, Don’t Know What to Say” into a monster hit that remains a classic today.

Teen idol Raymond Lauchengco found back-to-back success with “I Need You Back” and “Farewell”, while the iconic youth film Bagets was immortalized by its theme song “Growing Up”, performed by Gary Valenciano.

Her music also left an indelible mark on Philippine cinema. Sharon Cuneta, the country’s “Megastar”, recorded “To Love Again” as the theme for her movie of the same title.

Yet Odette’s artistry did not stop at writing for others. With hits like “Friend of Mine,” “You’re My Home,” “Dito Lang,” and “Wala Nang Nasa Isip”, she proved that she was also a captivating performer.

In recognition of her decades of influence, she received the Parangal Levi Celerio Lifetime Achievement Award in January 2024, and the Ryan Cayabyab Award at the 2024 Pinoy Playlist Music Festival.

My Favorite Story – A Dedication to Her Audience For Odette Quesada, every song is more than just music—it is a conversation with those who listen. “My Favorite Story” stands as one of her most personal dedications to her audience. Through its tender lyrics and timeless melody, she invites listeners to remember their own stories of love and life. Dedicated to her viewers, the song is a gift—an invitation to reflect, to feel, and to treasure the stories that shape who we are.

Odette Quesada is revered as a legendary pillar of Original Pilipino Music (OPM), a singer-songwriter whose prolific and emotionally resonant body of work has irrevocably defined the sound of love, longing, and romance in the Philippines. Her rise through Metropop in the early 1980s established her as a major force in the national music scene. The 1980s marked a golden era for her music, popularized by Ric Segreto, Raymond Lauchengco, Gary Valenciano, and Sharon Cuneta. Odette later emerged as a recording artist herself, showcasing a rare dual mastery—writing timeless songs and interpreting them with sincerity and emotional depth.

I don’t think there’s anyone in the world who’s listened to Odette Quesada’s music more than I have. Oftentimes, people say they grew up listening to her music. For me, her music is what I grew up with. Her music was the background to my every childhood memory. I don’t just listen to Odette Quesada, I know her. I know her songs by heart. I know the heartache behind every lyric. I know the joy behind every melody. I know the strength behind every word. I know the woman behind the music.

I cannot express how proud of my mom I am for everything she’s done. Growing up, I couldn’t easily share just how electrifying she felt when she got back on stage. It was undeniable. I got the huge reminder that her music wasn’t just something she did before — it was who she was. She is not just Odette Quesada the singer-songwriter. She is Odette Quesada the hardest working mom who moved heaven and earth to make sure I grew up wanting for nothing.

For me, my mom is my hero. She taught me how to stand my ground, how to use my voice, and how to love deeply. She taught me resilience, humility, and grace. I am endlessly grateful to be her daughter, and I am honored to witness her legacy. I know that one day, these words will no longer be enough to describe how much she means to me — but at least, I can immortalize my feelings here on print as she always knows how I feel.
